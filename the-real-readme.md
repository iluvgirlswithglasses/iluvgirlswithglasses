
Mine has been a life of triumphs. Well regarded, well rewarded. I might have been rewarded more than I deserve, tho.

But at the same time, there's never been a split second when I cease to doubt myself. In fact, in the very first place, I wanted to kms so much that I pushed myself into countless competitions just to feel alive. And even after all these successes, I still don't feel so alive. Even when I made it to national, it only felt great until it didn't anymore. For I must move on...

For I must move on, and face this suffocating, viscerally depressing, nihilistic nightmare again, until I draw my very last breath. As time marches, I'm more and more convinced in the meaninglessness of ambition, purpose, and self-actualization.

Sooner or later, I'll become a miserable, desperate being. *“If you’ve got a problem with the world, change yourself. If that’s a problem, close your eyes, shut your mouth, and live like a hermit”*. I've been drinking a plenty lately. But at least I'm not suicidal anymore. I learned it. And btw, being hermit doesn't sound like a bad life to me rn.
